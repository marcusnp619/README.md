Marcus N. Perez is an independent cybersecurity researcher, penetration tester, and author based in the United States. With a degree in Computer Science, he has built a career specializing in vulnerability discovery, ethical hacking, and modern bug‑bounty methodologies.

His work focuses on practical security education, clear technical communication, and advancing accessible knowledge for both new and experienced researchers alike. Marcus believes that understanding the foundations of how software fails is the single most powerful tool a security professional can carry.

Drawing from real‑world program experience across web applications, APIs, cloud architectures, and mobile clients, he bridges the gap between theoretical security concepts and hands‑on exploitation — making complex topics approachable without sacrificing depth or accuracy.

As an author, Marcus has channeled his field expertise into a first‑edition guide that serves as both a technical reference and a practical starting point for the next generation of security researchers. His mission: making the internet safer, one disclosed vulnerability at a time.

The Guide to the Fundamentals of Bug Bounties

📅 First Edition — 2026  •  Written by Marcus Perez

This comprehensive guide traces the complete evolution of bug bounty programs — from the first widely recognized structured initiative by Netscape in 1995 to the sophisticated platforms and government programs of today. It establishes why incentivized, authorized external testing consistently outperforms closed internal review alone.

Readers gain a deep understanding of how major technology organizations standardized scope definitions, rules of engagement, and reproducible reporting requirements — reducing legal ambiguity and enabling scalable triage workflows. The guide also explores how even government institutions adopted this model, including the landmark "Hack the Pentagon" program announced by Defense Secretary Ash Carter in March 2016.

From cloud‑native architectures and microservices to APIs and continuous deployment pipelines, the book equips researchers to tackle modern attack surfaces with clarity and confidence.

"Bug bounty programs have since transitioned from experimental initiatives into a core operational pillar of modern cybersecurity."

  • History & Evolution of Bug Bounty Programs
  • Program Structure: Scope, Rules & Disclosure Policies
  • Government Programs & National Security Applications
  • Modern Attack Surfaces: APIs, Cloud & Microservices
  • Writing Reproducible, High‑Signal Vulnerability Reports
  • Platform Workflows: HackerOne, Bugcrowd & Beyond
